🩺 Cervical Spine Injury — Plain Language Summary

Ronald Alexander (DOB: December 15, 1970) has been diagnosed with cervical spondylosis with radiculopathy — a pinched nerve in the neck — and degenerative disc disease of the cervical spine. These conditions cause constant, chronic pain in the neck and upper back, radiating into both shoulders and arms, with numbness, tingling, stiffness, and weakness. Symptoms developed gradually over two years and have progressively worsened. His physician, Dr. Lewis Rogatnick, manages his pain with prescription opioids and muscle relaxers. This condition has significantly limited his physical ability and disrupted his capacity to maintain prior employment.
